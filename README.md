AI-Augmented Corporate Credit & Valuation Screener
A quantitative finance pipeline that screens Nifty Metal & Mining stocks by combining Monte Carlo DCF valuation, a calibrated credit risk scorecard, peer-relative valuation, and GenAI-driven macro stress testing into a single composite Deal Score — validated against real forward returns.
What it does
Given a universe of stocks, the pipeline:
Values each company using a Monte Carlo-simulated DCF (2,000 simulations), with WACC computed from beta manually regressed against the Nifty 50 (rather than relying on `yfinance`'s default beta, which was found to be systematically understated for Indian equities)
Scores credit risk using a Logistic Regression model trained on 6,819 real companies' bankruptcy data, calibrated for reliable probability outputs and explained via SHAP
Ranks relative valuation (P/E, EV/EBITDA) only within statistically validated peer clusters (K-means), not across the whole sector blindly
Combines everything into a composite Deal Score
Backtests that score against actual 6-month forward returns to check whether it has any real predictive power
Stress-tests every valuation against 12 macro scenarios (recession, trade war, commodity crash, rate hikes, etc.) generated by an open-source LLM (Phi-3-mini), producing a resilience-adjusted score
Key technical decisions (and why)
This project involved real debugging, not just model-building. A few things worth highlighting:
`yfinance`'s beta field was wrong. Cross-checked against a manually regressed beta (3-year daily returns vs Nifty 50) and found `yfinance` understated beta by roughly 2–3x across the entire sector — likely computed against a non-Indian benchmark. This was corrected at the WACC calculation, not patched around.
Uniform growth assumptions don't work. An initial flat 8% FCF growth rate wildly overvalued mature, cyclical companies. Replaced with company-specific historical revenue CAGR (capped 2–12%).
Terminal value dominance guardrail. Added a check that excludes any DCF where terminal value exceeds 80–85% of total enterprise value — a sign the valuation is really just a bet on the WACC-growth spread, not the business.
Corporate action exclusion. Vedanta (VEDL) was excluded from the final screener after discovering its financial statements still reflected a pre-demerger combined entity (Vedanta completed a 5-way demerger in May 2026) while its market price reflected the post-demerger residual business — comparing the two would have been structurally invalid.
Credit feature scaling bug. The bankruptcy training dataset's ratio columns turned out to be pre-normalized to a 0–1 range, not raw ratios — live company ratios were being clipped to identical values. Fixed by rescaling live ratios into the training data's actual numeric range before inference.
Honest backtest result. The Deal Score showed a negative Spearman correlation (-0.80, not statistically significant at n=5) against 6-month forward returns — reported as-is rather than hidden, with the likely explanation being that DCF-based valuation targets long-term fundamental value, not short-term price momentum.
Tech stack
Languages/Libraries: Python, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, SHAP, SciPy
Data: yfinance, NSE public index data, Kaggle bankruptcy dataset
GenAI: Phi-3-mini (Microsoft), via Transformers/HuggingFace
Modeling: Logistic Regression (calibrated), K-Means clustering, Monte Carlo simulation

Limitations / Future work
Credit scoring restricted to non-financial companies; banks/NBFCs require sector-specific metrics (CAR, NPA ratios) not covered here
Backtest sample size (5 stocks) is too small for statistical significance — a larger universe and longer time horizon would be needed to validate the Deal Score's real predictive power
GenAI scenario generation currently uses a fixed prompt; could be extended to pull live macro news for more current inputs
